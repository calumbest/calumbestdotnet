Prompt: With OpenAI’s new raise at an $852B valuation, OpenAI Foundation’s stake is now worth $180B. Anthropic’s cofounders have pledged to donate 80% of their wealth. Nobody seems to have a concrete idea of how to deploy 100s of billions (soon trillions) of wealth productively to “make AI go well”. If you were in charge of the OpenAI Foundation right now, what exactly would you do? And when? It’s not enough to identify a cause you think is important, because that doesn’t answer the fundamental problem of how you convert money to impact. Identify the concrete strategy you recommend pursuing.

My answer:
If I was in charge of the OpenAI Foundation, I’d probably spend the next three months talking to a shit ton of people, paying a shit ton of money to get very smart generalists to work for me,  farming out large chunks of work to partner organizations, and thinking very very carefully about the principles that should inform our future work. The Foundation’s ultimate task will be worldview generation, information synthesis, and management, and that’s what they should try to do very well. If I had a lot of personal money (like, small billions) to give away because AI made me rich, I would avoid any organization that was taking a different approach. If an org told me today they had a plan for how to give away $1B or more, they’re either lying or dumb (note this isn’t true because TGF, so fix this).

I’d probably start giving away money to obviously good stuff, like R&D, data infrastructure, and talent development—these are areas where relatively small dollars can produce outsized gains, and where wasting time is especially painful—but I wouldn’t give huge sums away now.

Organizations optimize for their current level of funding — thinking 2x bigger is hard, 5x nearly impossible.

Is there any organization that can give you a good answer to the question of “How would I do 5x better if I had 5x more money, above some reasonable baseline?” I don’t think so. Most couldn’t do this. Individuals couldn’t, nonprofits can’t, most startups can’t.

This problem is most similar to being a government, with a constituency of the entire world. But, you need to avoid the failures of both autocratic and democratic governments. So we should do the good things that governments do and stop doing the bad things. For example, not paying enough for competent people, and succumbing to gridlock.

But governments don’t happen immediately, they’re incremental. Is there a good mental model for a supply *shock* going well?

It’s a waste of time to actually spend money on projects right now unless you’re doing pilots. Nobody’s thought enough about what’s going on.

If I had a lot of money to give away, I would give it to organizations that were following a plan like this. I would not give it to an organization that told me they had a clear plan for what to spend it on; that would be laughable and I’d ignore them.

I currently work at GiveWell, a private global health and development philanthropy that’s very invested for several reasons in the question of what the hell will happen with AI philanthropy (GW doesn’t endorse this essay). In part, my strong feeling that nobody has a plan comes from the reality that nobody has a plan for global health, which is a very small portion of possible philanthropy. I guarantee you no one has a plan for meta philanthropy.

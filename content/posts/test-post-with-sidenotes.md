This is a test post to make sure everything works. It has a few paragraphs of text and some footnotes that should render as sidenotes on wide screens.

The key insight behind effective writing is clarity.[^1] When you write clearly, readers can focus on your ideas rather than deciphering your prose. This is especially true for technical writing, where precision matters.

Another important principle is conciseness. Every sentence should earn its place.[^2] If a paragraph doesn't advance your argument or provide necessary context, cut it.

Good structure helps too. Use headings to create a clear hierarchy. Use short paragraphs to give readers breathing room. Use lists when you have three or more parallel items.

## A Section With More Notes

Sometimes you want to add context without derailing the main text.[^3] That's exactly what sidenotes are for — they let curious readers dig deeper without forcing everyone to follow a digression.

This post also tests the "Copy as Markdown" button below and the previous/next navigation.

[^1]: This is the first sidenote. On wide screens, it should appear in the right margin next to the paragraph that references it. On narrow screens, it falls back to a standard footnote at the bottom.

[^2]: Strunk & White's *The Elements of Style* remains the classic reference on concise writing, though it has its critics.

[^3]: Edward Tufte popularized sidenotes in his books on data visualization. He argues that footnotes and endnotes break the reader's flow, while sidenotes keep supplementary information visible alongside the main text.
